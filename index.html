Food Chain Visualisation

These visualisations are a glimpse into some of the flora and fauna within South American nature.

  • Jaguar Apex Predator

    Jaguars are solitary creatures, often preferring to hunt and roam alone in the dense jungles of Central and South America.

    • Ocelot Meso Predator

      Ocelots are nocturnal hunters, stealthily prowling through dense forests and grasslands in search of prey.

      • Quail Prey

        Quails are known for their secretive nature, often preferring to forage for food and nest in dense vegetation to stay hidden from predators.

      • Iguana Prey

        Iguanas are primarily herbivorous reptiles, known for their sunbathing behavior, which helps regulate their body temperature.
  • Harpy Eagle Apex Predator

    Harpy eagles are formidable predators of the South American rainforests, known for their solitary hunting habits and remarkable ability to snatch large prey from the treetops with incredible precision.
    • Opossum Meso Predator

      Opossums are nocturnal creatures, often venturing out at night to forage for a diverse diet that includes insects, fruits, and even small mammals.

      • Insects Prey

        Insects, as primary consumers, play a crucial role in transferring energy from plants to higher trophic levels in an ecosystem.
      • Crustaceans Prey

        Crustaceans play a crucial role in marine ecosystems as both predators and prey, helping maintain the balance of food chains by serving as a vital link between smaller organisms and larger marine species.
